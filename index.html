Algorithmic Statistics

An Online Learning Perspective on Modern Statistical Challenges

Workshop @ NeurIPS 2024

Scope

A number of recent studies have used methods and results from online learning to tackle statistical problems, such as hypothesis testing, generalisation in machine learning, and decision-making. Typically, these approaches work by dividing the problem into a stochastic and a worst-case part. The former can be dealt with by using martingale methods, while the latter can be related to the worst-case regret in an associated online game. The core observation is that an upper bound on the regret in the associated game is enough to deal with the problem at hand. Besides offering novel methods and perspectives to tackle these problems, the development of these kinds of approaches has also led to practical and concrete outcomes, such as stronger and more robust tests, tighter confidence intervals, and new generalisation bounds. These, in turn, can bring significant improvements in modern applications such as A/B testing, active experimentation, and personalised recommendation systems, which are of great interest to the NeurIPS community. However, a unifying framework connecting all the different efforts made in this direction is still missing. This workshop aims to showcase to the NeurIPS community how online learning theory can be used to tackle modern statistical challenges. In addition, we wish to bring together researchers from different fields to foster discussion and pave the way for new collaborations around this topic.
