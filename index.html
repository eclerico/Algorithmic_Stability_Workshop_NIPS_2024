Algorithmic Statistics

An Online Learning Perspective on Modern Statistical Challenges

Workshop @ NeurIPS 2024

Scope

A number of recent studies have focused on applying known online learning results and techniques to tackle statistical problems, such has hypothesis testing, generalisation in machine learning, and decision-making. Typically, these approaches involve dividing the problem into a probabilistic and a worst-case part. The former can be handled via martingale analysis tools, while the latter is related to the regret in an associated online game. The core idea is that the knowledge of a regret upper-bound in the associated game is then enough to deal with the problem at hand. Beside offering novel methods and perspectives to tackle these problems, the development of this kind of approaches has also led to practical and concrete outcomes, such as stronger and more robust tests, tighter confidence intervals, and new generalisation bounds. However, a unifying framework connecting all the different efforts that have been made in this direction is still missing. We hence believe that a workshop bringing together researchers interested in the topic could be highly beneficial.
